About Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

Hikkaduwa turtle hatcheries are important conservation centers located in Hikkaduwa, a coastal town in Sri Lanka. These hatcheries play a crucial role in protecting and preserving sea turtles, which are an endangered species. The primary goal of Hikkaduwa turtle hatcheries is to safeguard sea turtle eggs and ensure the survival of hatchlings. The hatcheries work closely with local communities, government agencies, and environmental organizations to raise awareness about the importance of sea turtle conservation and to combat threats to their population. The process at a Hikkaduwa turtle hatchery typically begins when volunteers and staff members patrol the nearby beaches during nesting season, which usually occurs between November and April. They carefully identify and mark sea turtle nests to prevent accidental damage or disturbance. When the nesting process is complete, the eggs are carefully collected from the beach and transported to the hatchery. This relocation is necessary to protect the eggs from natural predators and human interference. The eggs are handled with great care to ensure their viability. At the hatchery, the eggs are placed in specially constructed sandy nests or incubation chambers that replicate the natural conditions found on the beach. The temperature and humidity are carefully controlled to mimic the ideal conditions required for successful incubation. This process typically takes around 45 to 60 days, depending on the species of turtle. Once the eggs have reached the appropriate stage of development, trained staff members closely monitor the hatching process. As the hatchlings emerge from their shells, they are carefully released into large holding tanks or pools. These tanks are filled with seawater and provide a safe environment for the hatchlings to grow stronger before their release into the ocean. Before releasing the turtles, hatchery staff often educate visitors and tourists about the importance of sea turtle conservation and the threats facing these magnificent creatures. They emphasize the need for responsible behavior on the beaches, such as avoiding bright lights and minimizing disturbances during nesting season. When the hatchlings are deemed ready, they are released into the ocean during the evening or early morning hours. This timing increases their chances of survival, as they can navigate by moonlight towards the safety of the water. Hikkaduwa turtle hatcheries also engage in other important conservation activities. They rehabilitate injured or sick turtles, often those that have been affected by fishing nets or pollution. These injured turtles receive necessary medical treatment and care until they are healthy enough to be released back into the ocean. The hatcheries also conduct research and collect data on sea turtles, contributing to scientific knowledge about their behavior, migration patterns, and population dynamics. This information aids in the development of effective conservation strategies and helps raise awareness about the need to protect these incredible creatures. Overall, Hikkaduwa turtle hatcheries serve as vital centers for sea turtle conservation in Sri Lanka. They work tirelessly to protect endangered turtle species, raise awareness among locals and tourists, and actively contribute to the preservation of marine biodiversity.
